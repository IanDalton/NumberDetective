🧠 How to Play — Learn by Example

You're trying to guess a secret n-digit number. After each guess, you get a hint telling you:

  • How many digits are correct and in the right place.
  • How many digits are correct but in the wrong place.

Let’s walk through an actual game. The hidden number is ??? (we’ll reveal it at the end).

🔍 Guess 1: 943
🧾 Hint: 1 digit is correct and in the right place.
  • Digits: 9, 4, 3
  • Only 1 of them is right and in the right spot.
  • The other two? Trash.
🔍 Guess 2: 223
🧾 Hint: No digits are correct. Not even close.
  • Digits: 2, 2, 3
  • 100% wrong.
  • We now know: 2 and 3 are not in the answer.
  • That 3 from the first guess? Confirmed false alarm.
🔍 Guess 3: 989
🧾 Hint: 2 digits are correct and in the right place. 1 digit is correct but misplaced.
  • Digits: 9, 8, 9
  • Only two different digits: 9 and 8
  • If both 9s were correct and in place, we wouldn’t get a misplaced digit hint.
  • So:
    • One 9 is in the right place.
    • One 8 is in the right place.
    • The second 9? Right digit, wrong spot.
  • Conclusion: The answer is made of two 8s and one 9.
🎯 Answer: 988
📌 Summary
  • Track which digits show up across guesses.
  • Eliminate what doesn’t work.
  • Use the position clues to narrow it down.

Each guess is a riddle. Each hint is your breadcrumb.
